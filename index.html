PURPOSE OF STUDY

The purpose of this research study is to examine how you think about and experience aspects of your self.


SUBJECTS
Inclusion Requirements

You are eligible to participate in this study if you are at least 18 years of age or older, can read English, have good or corrected vision.


Time Commitment

This study will involve approximately 40 minutes of your time.


PROCEDURES

The following procedures will occur: you will read this form, complete the study, and receive a debriefing at the end of the study. The study will be completed on your own computer in your own physical space. We suggest that you may want to complete the study privately, so that others cannot view your responses. In the study you will complete a survey about your social experiences, and you may be asked to also complete a task on the computer where you will sort words using a computer mouse.


RISKS AND DISCOMFORTS

This study involves no more than minimal risk. There are no known harms or discomforts associated with this study beyond those encountered in normal daily life. During the study, you will be asked potentially sensitive questions. As such, you are advised to complete the study in a secluded place to maintain your privacy. If you experience any mental anguish as a result of the study, you are encouraged to contact the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration National Helpline, 1-800-662-HELP (4357), (also known as the Treatment Referral Routing Service) or TTY: 1-800-487-4889 . This is a confidential, free, 24-hour-a-day, 365-day-a-year, information service, in English and Spanish, for individuals and family members facing mental and/or substance use disorders.

Participating in this research in person carries risk of COVID-19 transmission. To mitigate these risks, the research assistants and all participants will be required to wear a high-quality mask. Shared equipment within the lab space will also be sanitized between study sessions. Additionally, the research team will adopt university, local, and state recommendations to ensure the safety of participants and members of the research team.


BENEFITS
Subject Benefits

You may not directly benefit from participation in this study.


Benefits to Others or Society

Through this researcher we are able to gain a better understanding of how people experience their social identities.

ALTERNATIVES TO PARTICIPATION

The only alternative to participation in this study is not to participate.


COMPENSATION, COSTS AND REIMBURSEMENT
Compensation for Participation

You will receive research credit at the rate of 1 credit per 10 minutes of participation.


WITHDRAWAL OR TERMINATION FROM THE STUDY AND CONSEQUENCES

You are free to withdraw from this study at any time. If you decide to withdraw from this study you should notify the research assistant immediately. The research team may also end your participation in this study if you do not follow instructions, miss scheduled visits, or if your safety and welfare are at risk.


CONFIDENTIALITY
Subject Identifiable Data

We will not collect any personally identifying information about you. We will not be able to link your data with you as an individual person.


Data Storage

All research data will be stored on a laptop computer that is password protected.


Data Access

The researcher named on the first page of this form will have access to your study records. Any information derived from this research project that personally identifies you will not be voluntarily released or disclosed without your separate consent, except as specifically required by law. Publications and/or presentations that result from this study will not include identifiable information about you.


Data Retention

The researchers intend to keep the research data in a repository indefinitely. Other researchers will have access to the data for future research.


Mandated Reporting

California law, the researcher is required to report known or reasonably suspected incidents of abuse or neglect of a child, dependent adult or elder, including, but not limited to, physical, sexual, emotional, and financial abuse or neglect. If any researcher has or is given such information in the course of conducting this study, she may be required to report it to the authorities.
